      Working in Downtown, particularly the southeast end of Downtown, leaves much to be desired during the lunch hour. The office ordered out at New Moon for the first time a few weeks ago, and there was a lot of commotion over a potential shifting of powers between the triumvirate of eateries in our area; namely a lunch truck, a taco stand, and a Persian restaurant up the street to whom which we send a larger Christmas basket every year. Now you have to remember that desperation will sometimes transform the dreariest lunch box into a tropical oasis fit for any Arabian sand dune, so with careful consideration I present my review. I wouldn't eat here unless I were at work. From 9 to 5, 4 days a week, I console myself into believing that the food is groovy. It is, after all, faintly reminiscent of authentic Chinese cuisine, prepared fresh, and delivered quickly. But the Kung Pao Chicken was tough. And the Tangerine Beef: meat that ain't half as tough as the batter that coats it. Also the Chinese Chicken Salad was tossed in soy sauce and not much else (OK so I exaggerate, but the flavors weren't very complex). The waitress on the phone went so far as to suggest a side of plum sauce to go with my salad... "Oh, is it supposed to come with plum sauce?" "No, but a lot of people like to order it that way." ...For reasons that became self-evident later, and I telepathically thanked her for the recommendation. I have to give them credit for making a good shrimp chow mein, but the food is most certainly not the culmination of hundreds of thousands of Chinese immigrants living in Los Angeles County. If there is even a Chinese employee in the entire joint. Maybe someone else can clarify.

        Working in Downtown, particularly the southeast end of Downtown, leaves much to be desired during the lunch hour. The office ordered out at New Moon for the first time a few weeks ago, and there was a lot of commotion over a potential shifting of powers between the triumvirate of eateries in our area; namely a lunch truck, a taco stand, and a Persian restaurant up the street to whom which we send a larger Christmas basket every year.

        Now you have to remember that desperation will sometimes transform the dreariest lunch box into a tropical oasis fit for any Arabian sand dune, so with careful consideration I present my review.

        I wouldn't eat here unless I were at work. From 9 to 5, 4 days a week, I console myself into believing that the food is groovy. It is, after all, faintly reminiscent of authentic Chinese cuisine, prepared fresh, and delivered quickly. But the Kung Pao Chicken was tough. And the Tangerine Beef: meat that ain't half as tough as the batter that coats it. Also the Chinese Chicken Salad was tossed in soy sauce and not much else (OK so I exaggerate, but the flavors weren't very complex). The waitress on the phone went so far as to suggest a side of plum sauce to go with my salad...

        "Oh, is it supposed to come with plum sauce?"

        "No, but a lot of people like to order it that way."

        ...For reasons that became self-evident later, and I telepathically thanked her for the recommendation.

        I have to give them credit for making a good shrimp chow mein, but the food is most certainly not the culmination of hundreds of thousands of Chinese immigrants living in Los Angeles County. If there is even a Chinese employee in the entire joint. Maybe someone else can clarify.
