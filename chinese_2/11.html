      I had been wanting to try this place for a while, and on a Saturday morning I finally got my chance. Parking is a small lot (but free, which is a blessing in Chinatown) and was unexpectedly full for being just after opening time. Once inside, the line was a few people long and was moving smoothly. The employees were all business, which can be misconstrued as rude service because they're not smiling or kissing your ass for tips, but that's the way things roll in most of Shanghai so it wasn't a surprise to me. Looking around inside of the restaurant, I got the impression that it had been there a long time, with some worn out areas (the tables and floors no longer have a bright shine to them), but overall very clean. The best part, of course, is the food. Not only was it considerably less expensive than places closer to my parents' house, is also served all day at prices much closer to what I paid in China... and the taste was there too. The were super moist and fluffy, the were juicy, and really hit the spot, but the egg custards... sorry, they were good but my heart is still with China's Lillian Bakery until I find one comparable to them. Although this place isn't easy for me to reach on a weekday after work, you'll likely find me here more often on a weekend from now on.

        I had been wanting to try this place for a while, and on a Saturday morning I finally got my chance. Parking is a small lot (but free, which is a blessing in Chinatown) and was unexpectedly full for being just after opening time.

        Once inside, the line was a few people long and was moving smoothly. The employees were all business, which can be misconstrued as rude service because they're not smiling or kissing your ass for tips, but that's the way things roll in most of Shanghai so it wasn't a surprise to me.

        Looking around inside of the restaurant, I got the impression that it had been there a long time, with some worn out areas (the tables and floors no longer have a bright shine to them), but overall very clean.

        The best part, of course, is the food. Not only was it considerably less expensive than places closer to my parents' house, is also served all day at prices much closer to what I paid in China... and the taste was there too. The were super moist and fluffy, the were juicy, and really hit the spot, but the egg custards... sorry, they were good but my heart is still with China's Lillian Bakery until I find one comparable to them.

        Although this place isn't easy for me to reach on a weekday after work, you'll likely find me here more often on a weekend from now on.
